The Great Teacher Rubric

A framework for professional growth across five domains. Use the tabs to explore each area — our department focus this term is highlighted.

Developing positive attitudes to learning

Pre-novice

  • Recognises a positive climate is a prerequisite for success
  • Understands factors that influence motivation
  • Knows how to develop self-esteem

Novice

  • Takes action to develop pupils' attitudes to learning
  • Ensures classroom is welcoming and motivating
  • Fosters positive pupil relationships through routines

Beginning

  • Uses a range of strategies to promote positive attitudes
  • Takes responsibility for developing pupils' self-management

Advanced

  • Consistent action to develop self-esteem and relationships with subject
  • Considered use of language to develop pupils' self-image

Establishing routines

Pre-novice

  • Can identify several behaviour management strategies

Novice

  • Routines established for entry, exit, start/end of lessons
  • Able to separate behaviour from the child

Beginning

  • Routines continually reviewed to maximise learning time

Advanced

  • Routines foster school ethos and teacher's own vision for learning

Managing the emotional charge

Pre-novice

  • Monitors internal feelings and how they present externally
  • Awareness of pupils' possible emotions

Novice

  • Aware of own state of mind and its effect on pupils
  • Manages outward expression of thoughts and feelings

Beginning

  • Manages own emotional state consistently
  • Deals appropriately with individual and group emotional needs

Advanced

  • Adapts teaching persona to each class context
  • Creates emotionally safe environment for risk-taking

Upholding policies & responding to behaviour

Pre-novice

  • Knows professional duty to enforce behaviour policies

Novice

  • Uses least intrusive intervention first
  • Responds to de-escalate after incidents

Beginning

  • Has planned escalation system for reward and sanction
  • Takes ownership of behaviour beyond own classroom

Advanced

  • Consistent use of cognitive domain of behaviour management
  • Early, adept interventions to prevent escalation

Creating rigorous, outcome driven lessons

Pre-novice

  • Can explain learning as a journey from A to B
  • Attempts objective-driven lesson plans

Novice

  • Creates rigorous, objective-driven lesson plans
  • Plans time for bite-sized practice of key skills

Beginning

  • High expectations demonstrated in challenging objectives
  • Sophisticated use of scaffolding for all pupils

Advanced

  • Multiple I/We/You sequences within a lesson for rapid progress

Planning for progress over time

Pre-novice

  • Can explain backward planning
  • Understands spiral curriculum theory

Novice

  • Plans backwards from end-of-unit assessment
  • Integrates homework into the learning sequence

Beginning

  • Deep commitment to rigorous planning based on curriculum and pupil needs

Advanced

  • Sophisticated use of milestones within medium-term plans
  • Balances new input, scaffolding, repetition and mastery

Meeting academic needs of all pupils

Pre-novice

    Pre-novice

    • Thinks of ways to help struggling pupils succeed

Novice

  • Adapts plans in response to formative assessment
  • Awareness of impact on pupils with SpLD

Beginning

  • Variety of strategies across medium-term plans for differentiated access

Advanced

  • Anticipates misconceptions and barriers at planning stage
  • Proactively integrates literacy objectives into medium-term plans

Preparation of resources

Pre-novice

  • Fastidious in preparation of materials
  • Refreshes subject knowledge as part of planning

Novice

  • Selects resources that engage pupils
  • Maintains a well-organised classroom

Beginning

  • Carries out scholarly activity as part of medium-term planning

Advanced

  • Classroom displays scaffold future learning
  • Research is integral to the planning process

I — Teacher exposition

Pre-novice

  • Shares objectives clearly and motivatingly
  • Uses pupil-friendly vocabulary
  • Uses visuals to reinforce key points

Novice

  • Matches exposition length to pupils' listening skills
  • Provides agenda for listening
  • Uses range of exemplification strategies

Beginning

  • Range of strategies for clarity and engagement
  • Makes links to prior knowledge throughout

Advanced

  • Develops metacognition through demonstration and modelling
  • Exposes pitfalls and how to avoid them

We — Collaborative learning

Pre-novice

  • Understands link between talking and thinking
  • Uses Bloom's Taxonomy for question design

Novice

  • Promotes discussion with open, higher order questions
  • Chunks lessons between teacher and pupil talk

Beginning

  • Probes initial responses to check real understanding
  • Targets questions by attainment level

Advanced

  • Judicious use of teacher and pupil talk to develop real dialogue
  • Uses shared activities as critical assessment opportunities

You — Independent practice & instructions

Pre-novice

  • Scans room to monitor compliance
  • Economy of language when giving instructions

Novice

  • Circulates during independent practice
  • Instructions are specific, concrete, sequential

Beginning

  • Intervenes at the point of error
  • Checking for understanding built into instructions

Advanced

  • Circulates to gather data informing next steps
  • Instructions set clear expectations for process and outcome

Assessing

Pre-novice

  • Aware of NC expectations in specialism
  • Understands importance of regular accurate marking

Novice

  • Has marking schedule ensuring regular feedback
  • Uses moderation to validate accuracy

Beginning

  • Regularly assesses during lessons with constructive feedback
  • Provides written feedback that facilitates dialogue

Advanced

  • Designs assessment structures within curriculum planning
  • Pupils play active role in their own assessment

Analysing

Pre-novice

  • Aware of types of data schools record
  • Knows difference between data and information

Novice

  • Systematically records results of pupil assessment
  • Uses records to track pupil progress over time

Beginning

  • Uses school data and national expectations to evaluate progress
  • Uses moderation to compare with similar groups

Advanced

  • Uses data tools to monitor achievement by groups (FSM, SEND, gender)
  • Question-level analysis to pinpoint remaining misconceptions

Adapting

Pre-novice

  • Understands data informs as well as measures teaching

Novice

  • Uses pupil data to inform learning environment and differentiation

Beginning

  • Assesses prior knowledge at start of unit and adjusts plans
  • Seeks specialist support for pupils not making adequate progress

Advanced

  • Develops well-informed intervention plans targeting underachievement
  • Prioritises by urgency and potential impact

Self-management — the full progression

Pre-novice

  • Openly expresses belief in all children's ability
  • Personally organised
  • Identifies and expresses own emotions appropriately

Novice

  • Active participant in training and CPD
  • Uses systems for effective time management
  • Pro-actively uses strategies to reduce emotional reactions
  • Demonstrates humility

Beginning

  • Deep sense of responsibility as agent of change
  • Proactively sets challenging goals and seeks development
  • Effectively manages emotions in challenging situations
  • Balances confidence with modesty

Advanced

  • Holds self to account for the success of all pupils
  • Rigorously evaluates own impact to identify highest-leverage goals
  • Prioritises time by long and short-term impact on students
  • Robust professional persona; reflects on underlying reasons for feelings

Evidence informed practice — the full progression

Pre-novice

  • Develops knowledge from suggested texts
  • Ensures assignments impact classroom practice

Novice

  • Makes attempts to understand and adjust future approaches
  • Understands key general principles governing classroom practice

Beginning

  • Independent critical consumer of education policy and research
  • Able to use action research to improve classroom impact

Advanced

  • Conducts rigorous action research using data
  • Makes recommendations beyond own classroom
  • Reflections are both theory and practice based

Collaboration with colleagues & community — the full progression

Pre-novice

  • Expresses faith in abilities of others
  • Recognises value of engaging with parents and community
  • Positively receptive to support

Novice

  • Champions others and actively supports their goals
  • Seeks support from experienced colleagues professionally
  • Responds positively to requests for support from others

Beginning

  • Promotes positive staff culture by assuming the best of colleagues
  • Notices issues beyond own classroom and takes initiative
  • Considers what they might be overlooking in a situation

Advanced

  • Appropriately challenges those who are openly negative about others
  • Empathy with challenges faced by others; adjusts behaviour accordingly
  • Actively seeks ways to support colleagues beyond what is normally expected

Department reflection — where are you right now?

I actively champion colleagues and support them in achieving their goals — not just when asked, but as a habit.

When I have a concern or problem, I raise it directly with the right person rather than discussing it with others first.

I try to assume the best of colleagues — I give people the benefit of the doubt before forming a judgement about their decisions or actions.

I notice issues beyond my own classroom and take initiative to help resolve them, rather than waiting to be asked.

I manage my own emotional reactions in difficult situations — I reflect on what I feel without letting it drive my behaviour in the moment.

I am open to professional development — I actively seek out learning opportunities rather than waiting for them to come to me.

I hold myself to account for the success of all my pupils — not just the ones I find easiest to teach.
